About the Book

You are not broken.
You are not failing.

The sleepless newborn period — those raw, overwhelming first twelve months — is one of the most emotionally complex experiences a human being can walk through. Most parenting books skim past this reality. This one stops and sits in it with you.

Every chapter is built around emotional truth. You will find practical tools — routines, calming strategies, guidance on building support. But above all, you will find permission. Permission to feel what you feel. Permission to ask for help. Permission to be imperfect and exhausted and deeply human.

The midnight hours are long. But you will not walk through them alone.

For every parent who has ever sat alone in the dark, holding a tiny life, running on empty — this book is your hand to hold.

Inside the Book

Six Chapters of Emotional Truth

01
Understanding Your Baby’s Sleep
What’s normal, what’s a phase, and what science says. Your baby’s sleep is not broken — and neither are you.
02
Your Emotions Matter
Navigating anxiety, guilt, overwhelm, and the grief of exhaustion. You can love your baby completely and still find this genuinely hard.
03
Bonding Through the Night
Turning the hardest hours into moments of deep connection. Bonding is not an event — it is a slow, cumulative process.
04
Gentle Routines That Work
Calming strategies for your baby and for yourself. A routine is a set of gentle signals — not a rigid timetable.
05
Asking for Help
Building your support system without shame or guilt. Asking for help is the bravest, most loving thing you can do.
06
The Light at the End
Celebrating small wins and finding your rhythm as a family. It will change. It already is, even when you cannot see it.

Shumani Phaswana

Shumani Phaswana is the author of The Midnight Parent, a guide born from the belief that the emotional experience of new parenthood deserves as much attention as any practical sleep schedule or feeding routine.

Written with honesty, warmth, and deep respect for the reality of those first twelve months, this book offers something most parenting resources don’t: permission to be fully, imperfectly human.
